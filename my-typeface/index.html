Without typography, a text has no visual characteristics. A goblet can be invisible because the wine is not. But text is already invisible, so typography cannot be.

Rather than wine in a goblet, a more apt parallel might be helium in a balloon: the balloon gives shape and visibility to something that otherwise cannot be seen.

Matthew Butterick, 2016

It’s easy to learn the skills to produce good typography. Beyond that, you need only the ability to form opinions about typography. And everyone who reads—even kids—can do this.

What are the elements we call "serif"? Can you find them in this letter a? Tip: they turn into white.

Typography matters because it helps conserve the most valuable resource you have as a writer—reader attention.
